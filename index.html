NO₂

NO₂ mainly comes from traffic and gas boilers. Levels are higher near busy roads. Breathing high NO₂ for long periods can irritate the lungs and worsen asthma.

PM 2.5

PM2.5 is very tiny dust in the air. It comes from vehicle exhaust, construction, and burning fuels. These particles can enter the lungs and harm heart and lung health.

The Indoor Air Quality Risk

Indoor air is not always cleaner than outdoor air. Cooking, gas stoves, and poor ventilation can cause pollution to build up indoors. Opening windows can help, but not when outdoor pollution is high. Choosing the right time to ventilate is important.
